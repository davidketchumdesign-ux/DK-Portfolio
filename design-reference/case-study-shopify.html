E-Commerce · Case Study

Rebuilding the IA and design system behind a premium e-commerce experience.

A mobile-first Shopify redesign built on a new atomic design system — reworking information architecture, navigation, and a custom product configurator for a premium sporting goods retailer.

Role
Lead UX Designer

01 / 05
Empathize

The engagement opened with a discovery phase focused on understanding the client's current digital landscape and user base before proposing any solutions. Rather than jumping to redesign, the goal was to build a clear, evidence-based picture of what was and wasn't working.

Working with the broader team, I helped lead a current-state website audit, an accessibility evaluation, a competitive analysis, and a heuristics audit — surfacing friction points across navigation, mobile usability, and content structure.

Current State Audit Accessibility Evaluation Competitive Analysis Heuristics Audit
02 / 05
Define

Research findings were synthesized into proto-personas representing the site's core user types, used to guide every design decision that followed. The audits made clear that the existing information architecture — especially primary navigation — was a major source of friction, particularly on mobile.

How Might We

How might we restructure the site's navigation and product discovery flow so mobile shoppers can find and configure products as easily as desktop users?

03 / 05
Ideate

With the problem clearly defined, I led the creation of an atomic design system tailored specifically for Shopify's technical constraints — building reusable components from the smallest UI elements up through full page templates, so the system could scale across the entire site.

In parallel, I redesigned the site's information architecture, with primary navigation as the centerpiece — restructuring how product categories, collections, and configurator entry points were organized and surfaced.

04 / 05
Prototype

I brought the design system and new IA to life across the site's core templates — Home, Category (L1), Subcategory (L2), and Product (L3) pages — along with a custom product configurator built to handle the complexity of the client's product line, and revamped product listing and detail pages.

Every screen was built mobile-first and validated against Shopify's technical constraints from the start, so nothing designed would need to be re-engineered during handoff.

05 / 05
Test & Iterate

Designs were reviewed and refined with stakeholders and a third-party brand agency to ensure the new IA and component system stayed aligned with a refreshed visual identity. I also worked directly with the client's internal design team, walking them through Figma best practices and how to apply the new system going forward — so the design system would keep working long after handoff.
